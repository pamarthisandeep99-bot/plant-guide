Common Plant Diseases

A comprehensive guide to identifying, preventing, and treating the most common issues affecting your plants.

Powdery Mildew

Symptoms

White or gray powdery spots or patches on the upper surfaces of leaves, stems, and sometimes flowers. Leaves may turn yellow, curl, or drop prematurely.

Causes

Caused by various fungi species. It thrives in environments with high humidity, poor air circulation, and moderate temperatures. Unlike many fungi, it does not need standing water to grow.

Prevention

  • Ensure adequate spacing between plants for good air circulation.
  • Prune overcrowded branches to let light and air in.
  • Avoid overhead watering; water at the base of the plant.
  • Place plants in areas with sufficient sunlight.

Treatment Suggestions

Remove and destroy severely infected plant parts. Do not compost them. Apply a fungicide containing sulfur, potassium bicarbonate, or neem oil. A homemade spray of 1 tablespoon baking soda and 1/2 teaspoon liquid soap in a gallon of water can also be effective if applied early.

Root Rot

Symptoms

Plants appear wilted despite moist soil. Leaves turn yellow and drop off. Growth is stunted. If you check the roots, they will look dark brown or black and feel mushy or slimy, often with a foul odor.

Causes

Overwatering is the primary cause, which suffocates the roots. This creates a perfect environment for soil-borne fungi (like Pythium or Phytophthora) to attack the weakened root system.

Prevention

  • Never overwater. Always check soil moisture before watering.
  • Use pots with drainage holes. Never let plants sit in waterlogged saucers.
  • Use well-draining potting mixes containing perlite or coarse sand.

Treatment Suggestions

Take the plant out of its pot and wash away the soil from the roots. Trim away all black, mushy roots with sterilized scissors until only firm, white roots remain. Discard the old soil and wash the pot thoroughly with a bleach solution before repotting in fresh, dry soil. Withhold watering for a few days to allow roots to heal.

Leaf Spot Disease

Symptoms

Brown, black, yellow, or water-soaked spots on leaves. As the disease progresses, the spots may enlarge and merge. Severely infected leaves may yellow and drop.

Causes

Can be caused by either bacteria or fungi. Fungal leaf spots are more common and are spread by splashing water, wind, or contaminated tools.

Prevention

  • Keep the foliage dry by watering the soil directly, not the leaves.
  • Water early in the day so any splashed water on leaves dries before nightfall.
  • Clean up and dispose of fallen debris around the plant.

Treatment Suggestions

Immediately remove and discard infected leaves. Isolate the plant if possible. For severe fungal infections, a copper-based fungicide or neem oil can be applied following package instructions. Ensure the plant is not kept in overly humid, stagnant air.
